About the LiftPeople Project

What is this Project About?

The LiftPeople Project is our response to help curb the spread of COVID'19 and its aftermath impact on the lives of vulnerable Nigerians.

What are your causes targetted at?

Basically, we the essence of this project is to mobilize tailors within communities to sew and sterilize nose masks. These products would then be distributed to individuals within the commnities, either via markets, shops or their homes.

What are the impacts of using nose masks made from materials in the fight againts this pandemic.

Studies have shown that masks fundamentally prevent the transmission from you to others, sneezing, coughing, but also breathing. We believe that any precautions during this period is very useful in protecting individuals from infection.

So, what are the plans put in place for the mobilization, manufacturing and logistics of these masks.

Presently, we are working with a small test team of 6 tailors within the Lagos island, to sew nose masks. An initial agreement has been made with them to subsidize the costs of the masks. Furthermore, we are utilizing our personal funds and social capital to pay for the first masks that would be distributed.

What Logistics channel are you proposing to implement.

We aim to utilize the vast strength of the church in Lagos, partnering with them to distribute these masks to everyone within their congregation that might reqire it. Most churches are already providing relief materials to members that are most in needs. We also plan on sharing these masks in markets, shops that would be opened according to the guidelines of the state.

More Questions about the Cause

Tell me more about the provision of nose masks.

As earlier explaned, Studies have shown that masks fundamentally prevent the transmission from you to others, sneezing, coughing, but also breathing. We believe that any precautions during this period is very useful in protecting individuals from infection. The aim of this cause is to provide nose masks to everyone that cannot afford it, starting from Lagos, then to surrounding environs in Nigeria.

Wouldn't it be hard to breathe in these masks?

This is why all tailors in the LiftPeople Project are being tasked to use a material that would enable proper breathing practices. We are also making provisions for materials that would be used by the tailors after sewing to properly disinfect these masks. Finally, before the masks go out, we would also be properly sterilizing each masks, thereby reducing the possibility of transmission.

What other causes is the LiftPeople project involved in?

We believe that the production of nose masks is an avenue for job creation for tailors who have lost clients due to this pandemic. Providing a sense of obligation to them in the production of these masks, who enable them generate a source of income in this period. We also aim to provide relief materials for families intensely hit by this pandemic in manner of funds, food or shelter.
